How Tessera works, and how to get help.

Recording & marking

Tessera records continuously while open and mounted. There are three ways an incident becomes a dossier:

  • Automatic. The Incident Engine watches motion and detects collisions and hard braking. Three sensitivity presets — Relaxed, Standard, Sensitive — tune how readily it fires.
  • One tap. The MARK button flags the moment by hand.
  • “Hey Siri, mark this in Tessera.” Hands-free, even with the screen dark.

Each mark captures the footage from before and after the moment, so the lead-up is always preserved.

Verifying a dossier

Every signed dossier exports as a bundle anyone can check — no Tessera app required. With the public key, video, and signature files in hand:

  • openssl dgst -sha256 -verify pubkey.pem -signature core.sig core.json

A result of Verified OK proves the telemetry has not been altered. The hashes printed in the included PDF report match the bundle’s files exactly, and the clip carries an embedded C2PA content credential. Editing a single video frame or telemetry sample breaks verification.

Free tier & the one-time unlock

The free tier records continuously and saves plain video clips from your marks. The one-time $9.99 unlock adds the Incident Engine: automatic detection, signed and verifiable dossiers, the AI narrative, and court-ready export. It is a single purchase, with Family Sharing — no subscription.

Tips

  • Mount the phone with a clear view of the road; Tessera records what the camera sees.
  • On hot days, a shaded or vented mount helps — Tessera automatically lowers frame rate and resolution before heat forces a stop, and keeps recording.
  • Tessera arms itself each time you open it. There is no account and nothing to configure to start.
